# Short-Form Video

Design for a specific viewer promise and observable behavior, not generic virality formulas.

## Workflow

1. Define the target viewer, platform, content job, desired action, evidence, and production constraints.
2. Choose one promise and one format: demonstration, explanation, story, original reaction, comparison, challenge, breakdown, or serial narrative.
3. Write a truthful opening that establishes tension, utility, novelty, or stakes without bait-and-switch.
4. Structure beats for continuous progress: setup, proof or demonstration, turn, payoff, and optional next action.
5. Produce a time-coded script, shot list, on-screen text, B-roll, sound, captions, safe-area notes, and edit rhythm.
6. Remove dead time, repeated setup, unsupported claims, and elements that do not advance comprehension or emotion.
7. Define variants that change one material variable and a measurement plan using retention curves and downstream actions.

## Guardrails

- Verify current platform format, music, copyright, disclosure, and monetization rules before publishing.
- Do not reuse third-party clips without rights and meaningful transformation.
- Do not fabricate events, expertise, reactions, results, or social proof.
- Avoid flashing, unsafe challenges, deceptive edits, and inaccessible captions.

## Output

Return the viewer promise, format, time-coded script, shot/edit plan, asset list, disclosure needs, variants, success measures, and production checklist.
