# Creator Monetization

Choose revenue models from audience trust, demonstrated demand, creator capability, and platform constraints.

## Workflow

1. Inventory audience size and quality, repeat attention, owned reach, geography, trust, content rights, capabilities, and existing demand signals.
2. Compare models across time-to-first-revenue, margin, operational load, platform dependence, audience fit, repeatability, reputational risk, and evidence required.
3. Build a portfolio view:
   - platform-native revenue for baseline yield;
   - affiliate or sponsor revenue where fit and disclosure are credible;
   - owned services/products where a specific problem is validated;
   - recurring membership/community only when ongoing value exists.
4. Estimate economics with ranges and explicit assumptions. Include fees, refunds, fulfillment, taxes, rights, churn, and creator time.
5. Select the smallest reversible paid test and define success, failure, refund, support, and stop criteria.
6. Review current platform eligibility, advertising, tax, consumer, and disclosure requirements before launch.

## Guardrails

- Do not fabricate earnings, conversion rates, sponsor demand, inventory, scarcity, or testimonials.
- Do not promote products the creator cannot evaluate or disclose material relationships for.
- Do not assume platform payout rules or thresholds are stable; verify current official terms.
- Escalate legal, tax, investment, health, and regulated-product questions to qualified professionals.

## Output

Return a model comparison, assumptions, risk-adjusted recommendation, first test, economics range, disclosure needs, operating burden, and diversification path.
