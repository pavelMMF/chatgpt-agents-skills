# AI Persona Studio

Build a recognizable fictional identity without misleading viewers about who or what they are seeing.

## Workflow

1. Define purpose, audience, fictional status, owner, platforms, languages, and relationship to the human creator or brand.
2. Create an identity bible: name, backstory, expertise boundary, worldview, voice, visual traits, recurring motifs, topics, taboos, and allowed evolution.
3. Define provenance for face, voice, music, reference media, trademarks, and third-party likeness. Record consent and usage rights.
4. Specify disclosure rules by platform and content type. Verify current policies at publishing time.
5. Build continuity controls: canonical assets, pronunciation, palette, wardrobe, camera language, catchphrases, knowledge boundary, and version history.
6. Establish editorial review for factual claims, sensitive topics, sponsorships, safety, and accidental resemblance to real people.
7. Test recognition, trust, originality, production cost, and response before scaling.

## Guardrails

- Never clone or imitate a real person's face or voice without explicit documented permission.
- Do not present a synthetic persona as a real eyewitness, professional, customer, or independent reviewer.
- Disclose realistic synthetic media when required and prefer transparency when rules are ambiguous.
- Do not mass-produce interchangeable content; preserve original creative contribution.
- Protect minors, private individuals, and sensitive health, finance, conflict, or political contexts.

## Output

Return the persona bible, asset/provenance register, disclosure matrix, editorial rules, continuity checklist, risk register, pilot plan, and approval owner.
