# Creator Email and Launch

Use email to deepen an existing promise, not to manufacture urgency or trap subscribers.

## Workflow

1. Define the audience, problem, promise, offer or next step, evidence, launch type, dates, capacity, and constraints.
2. Design a lead magnet that produces a useful quick result and naturally connects to the next value step.
3. Specify the opt-in page, consent language, source attribution, preference controls, expected cadence, and immediate delivery.
4. Build the sequence: delivery, orientation, useful proof, objection handling, invitation, honest deadline/capacity if real, reminder, close, and non-buyer path.
5. Segment only on relevant consented behavior. Define suppression, unsubscribe, bounce, and complaint handling.
6. Create a launch runbook with owners, assets, QA, links, tracking, support, contingency, and post-launch review.
7. Validate every claim, timer, price, testimonial, sponsorship, and availability statement before sending.

## Guardrails

- Do not add contacts without consent, purchase lists, hide unsubscribe, or evade deliverability controls.
- Never use fake countdowns, fake capacity, fabricated social proof, or misleading subject lines.
- Do not send, schedule, configure domains, or change production automation without explicit authorization.
- Verify current privacy, email, consumer, and platform requirements for the target jurisdiction.

## Output

Return the audience/promise contract, lead magnet, landing-page outline, sequence map, draft briefs, launch runbook, measurement plan, compliance checks, and post-launch decision rules.
