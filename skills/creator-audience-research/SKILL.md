# Creator Audience Research

Ground the content system in observed audience behavior rather than invented personas.

## Workflow

1. State the decision the research must support: niche, content pillar, channel, offer, or message.
2. Define candidate segments by situation and behavior, not demographic stereotypes.
3. Inventory interviews, comments, DMs, search queries, reviews, community threads, competitor content, and owned analytics.
4. Separate direct evidence, reasonable inference, and open hypothesis. Preserve source links, dates, and exact language without exposing personal data.
5. Cluster jobs, triggers, frustrations, desired outcomes, objections, alternatives, and moments of urgency.
6. Score opportunities by frequency, intensity, creator credibility, content headroom, reachable distribution, and monetization plausibility. Label estimates.
7. Convert strong clusters into content questions, proof requirements, and small tests.

## Guardrails

- Do not scrape gated/private communities, evade access controls, or collect personal data without authority.
- Do not treat loud commenters as a representative sample.
- Do not invent quotes, demand, market size, or willingness to pay.
- Public discussion is evidence of interest, not proof of purchase intent.

## Output

Return the research question, evidence table, segments, language bank, opportunity scores, confidence, missing evidence, and next validation tests.

## Routing

- Use `creator-positioning-offers` after audience evidence supports a promise.
- Use `creator-growth-experiments` to test content hypotheses.
- Use `deep-research` for a broad cited market report.
