# Creator Positioning and Offers

Improve the underlying promise before polishing copy.

## Workflow

1. Gather audience evidence, creator capabilities, constraints, alternatives, and existing proof.
2. Define the target situation, costly problem, desired change, mechanism or perspective, and reasons to believe.
3. Produce three positioning options: narrow/high-relevance, broader/flexible, and simplest common-path message.
4. Test clarity, distinctiveness, credibility, content depth, reachable audience, and extension potential.
5. Design an offer ladder only from validated needs: free utility, low-risk entry, core transformation, continuity, and optional premium help.
6. Specify scope, deliverables, prerequisites, exclusions, support, timeline, price logic, risk reversal, and evidence gaps.
7. Create a proof plan using demonstrations, samples, transparent case evidence, or measurable pilots.

## Ethical Conversion Rules

- Never fabricate testimonials, revenue, credentials, client counts, scarcity, urgency, guarantees, or comparison results.
- Do not promise outcomes outside the creator's control.
- State material limitations, sponsorships, affiliate relationships, and AI involvement where relevant.
- Prefer a clear fit/no-fit boundary over pressure tactics.

## Output

Return positioning options, recommendation, audience/promise/proof contract, content pillars, offer ladder, exclusions, proof backlog, risks, and validation plan.
