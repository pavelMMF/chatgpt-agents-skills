# Creator Growth Experiments

Replace vague “post more” advice with falsifiable tests and decision rules.

## Workflow

1. Define the objective and funnel stage: qualified reach, retained attention, return viewers, follows, owned-audience capture, or revenue.
2. Establish the baseline, observation window, content unit, audience segment, and data limitations.
3. Write one hypothesis with mechanism, expected direction, primary metric, guardrails, and stop rule.
4. Change one major variable where practical: topic, promise, first frame, length, format, packaging, distribution, or call to action.
5. Track exposure and downstream behavior. Use platform-native retention and conversion evidence, not engagement alone.
6. Compare like with like, note algorithm and seasonality confounds, and avoid causal claims from tiny uncontrolled samples.
7. Decide: repeat, refine, expand, archive, or collect more evidence. Add learning to a durable experiment ledger.

## Guardrails

- Never buy fake engagement, automate spam, evade platform limits, or coordinate deceptive activity.
- Do not optimize for outrage, misinformation, unsafe behavior, or misleading thumbnails.
- Do not treat a single viral post as a stable strategy.
- Do not schedule publishing or spend money without explicit authorization.

## Output

Return the baseline, hypothesis, variant matrix, production scope, metrics, guardrails, analysis method, stop rule, decision, and next test.

- Use `data-analysis` for deeper channel data work.
